Telegraph Museum

Established in 1876 as the Telegraph Office at the end of the Taiping-Port Weld railroad, it later evolved into the Posts & Telegraph Department in 1885, offering telegraph, telephone, postal, and banking servicesâ€”the first in the Malay States to do so. Through the Japanese occupation and the post-war era, this colonial gem witnessed significant transformations in its services. Today, it stands as a cherished landmark, showcasing artefacts, photos, and stories that trace the evolution of telecommunications. Don't miss the courtyard featuring plants integral to Malaysia's telegraph history.

Taiping Lake Garden

Nestled in the heart of Taiping, this verdant oasis offers a tranquil escape with its scenic lakes, lush greenery, and charming bridges. Originally a mining ground, it was transformed into a picturesque park featuring diverse flora, fauna, and recreational facilities over the years. The gardens hold historical significance as a testament to Taiping's commitment to preserving nature amidst urban development. Perfect for leisurely strolls, picnics, or simply unwinding amidst nature's embrace.
